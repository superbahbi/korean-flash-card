# Goyo Learning Guide

Welcome to Goyo, your personal Korean language learning companion. This guide explains how to use the app effectively and maximize your learning outcomes.

## 🎯 How Goyo Works

### The Spaced Repetition System (SRS)

Goyo uses a scientifically-proven learning method called **Spaced Repetition**. Here's how it works:

1. **You learn a word** by studying it in the app.
2. **The app shows you that word again** at strategic intervals (1 day, 2 days, 4 days, 7 days, etc.).
3. **Each time you review**, you tell the app how well you remembered it:
   - **Again**: You forgot it → Review tomorrow
   - **Good**: You remembered it → Review in 2 days
   - **Easy**: You knew it instantly → Review in 4 days

This pattern ensures you review words **just before you forget them**, maximizing memory retention.

### Why This Works

Research by psychologist Hermann Ebbinghaus showed that our brains naturally forget information over time. By reviewing at the right moments, you can **dramatically extend how long you remember** a word—from hours to months to years.

## 🎮 Gamification: Stay Motivated

### Daily Streaks 🔥

A **streak** is how many consecutive days you've practiced. The longer your streak, the more committed you are to learning!

- Practice every day to build your streak.
- Missing a day resets your streak (but you can start a new one tomorrow).
- Challenge yourself to reach 7, 30, or 100-day streaks!

### XP & Levels ⭐

Every card you review earns you **Experience Points (XP)**:
- **Again**: 5 XP
- **Good**: 10 XP
- **Easy**: 15 XP

As you accumulate XP, you level up! Higher levels show your commitment and progress.

### Daily Goals 🎯

The app suggests reviewing **10 cards per day**. This is the sweet spot:
- **Not too much**: Prevents burnout
- **Not too little**: Maintains consistent progress

## 🎤 Multisensory Learning

### Text-to-Speech (TTS)

Goyo includes **native Korean pronunciation** for every word. Hearing the word while seeing it creates stronger memories through **Dual Coding Theory**.

- The app automatically plays pronunciation when you start a new card.
- Click the **speaker icon** to replay pronunciation anytime.
- Hearing the word helps you recognize it when spoken in real life.

### Why Hearing Matters

Language is primarily **auditory**. By combining:
- **Visual** (Hangul text)
- **Phonetic** (Romanization)
- **Auditory** (Native pronunciation)

...you create multiple neural pathways for the same word, making it **much harder to forget**.

## 💡 Study Tips for Success

### 1. **Study Every Day**
- Consistency beats intensity. 10 minutes daily > 2 hours once a week.
- Your streak will motivate you to keep going.

### 2. **Use "Easy" Strategically**
- Only click "Easy" if you knew the answer *instantly* (within 1-2 seconds).
- If you hesitated, click "Good" instead.
- This keeps your SRS intervals accurate.

### 3. **Pay Attention to Example Sentences**
- Each word includes a contextual sentence.
- Understanding how words are used in context helps you remember them better.

### 4. **Engage with Pronunciation**
- Don't skip the audio! Hearing native pronunciation is crucial.
- Try to mimic the pronunciation yourself.

### 5. **Review Regularly**
- The app will remind you when cards are due.
- Don't skip reviews—they're the core of the learning process.

## 🎓 Learning Science Behind Goyo

Goyo is built on three pillars of research:

### 1. Spaced Repetition (Ebbinghaus, 1885)
Reviewing at expanding intervals maximizes retention.

### 2. Active Recall (Roediger & Karpicke, 2006)
Retrieving information from memory is 2-3x more effective than passive review.

### 3. Comprehensible Input (Krashen, 1985)
Learning language through context and multiple modalities (text, sound, meaning) accelerates acquisition.
